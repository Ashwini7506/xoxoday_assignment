# Content Humanization & Readability

## Why Humanization Matters for SEO (2025–2026)

Google's March 2024 core update merged the Helpful Content System into its core
ranking algorithm. Content that sounds like it was written by a committee, a
template, or an AI without human editing now gets suppressed — not penalized, but
simply not surfaced. Human-sounding, specific, experience-backed writing is the
new baseline.

**Key statistics:**
- Pages with a Flesch Reading Ease score of 60–70 get 40% more time-on-page
- First-person specificity ("we tested this with 42 clients") increases AI citation
  rate by 2.3× vs. generic claims
- Sentences over 25 words reduce comprehension by 32% (Nielsen Norman Group)
- Passive voice above 10% correlates with bounce rate increase on B2B pages

## The Humanization Checklist

### 1. Voice & Tone

**Corporate/AI signals to eliminate:**
- "We are committed to delivering end-to-end solutions"
- "Our robust platform enables seamless integration"
- "Leverage our best-in-class capabilities"
- "In today's fast-paced business landscape"
- "It is worth noting that..."
- "Furthermore," / "Moreover," / "Additionally,"
- Passive voice > 10% of sentences

**Human signals to add:**
- Direct subject-verb sentences: "This platform connects X to Y"
- Named, specific outcomes: "Cut reporting time from 4 hours to 20 minutes"
- Acknowledging friction: "This part is hard. Here's what helps."
- First-person plural where genuine: "We learned this after running 200 programs"
- Contractions where appropriate: "don't", "it's", "you'll"
- Rhetorical questions that readers are actually asking: "So who pays the sales rep?"

### 2. Sentence Structure

**Targets:**
| Metric | Target | Warning Zone |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Average sentence length | 15–20 words | > 25 words |
| Paragraph length | 2–4 sentences | > 5 sentences |
| Passive voice % | < 10% | > 15% |
| Flesch Reading Ease | 60–70 (general B2B) | < 50 |
| Grade level | 9–11 (B2B) | > 14 |

**Fix patterns:**
- Split sentences at "which", "that", "however", "although" — make them two sentences
- Move the subject to the front: "Rewards are distributed by the platform" → "The platform distributes rewards"
- Replace nominalizations: "provide facilitation of" → "help with"; "achieve optimization of" → "optimize"

### 3. Specificity Over Generality

| Generic (remove) | Specific (replace with) |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| "many customers" | "42 of our enterprise clients" |
| "significant improvement" | "34% increase in rep activation" |
| "various industries" | "CPG, pharma, and fintech teams" |
| "our platform helps" | "the dashboard shows each rep their rank in real time" |
| "increased engagement" | "reps logged in 3× more often in the first month" |
| "proven results" | "verified by a 2024 Forrester study of 180 loyalty programs" |

### 4. Opening Lines (First 40 Words)

The opening must answer: "What is this? Who is it for? What will I get?"

**Bad opening (corporate):**
> "In today's highly competitive consumer goods landscape, brands are increasingly
> looking for innovative ways to engage their diverse stakeholder ecosystem across
> the value chain."

**Good opening (human):**
> "Running a loyalty program for a consumer goods brand means managing six different
> types of people — distributors, retailers, sales reps, merchandisers, influencers,
> and consumers — all with different motivations. This page covers how to reward each one."

**Rules:**
- No "In today's [adjective] world/landscape/environment" openings — ever
- Answer the implied question in the first sentence
- Name the reader's role or problem in the first 20 words
- Lead with the outcome, not the product

### 5. Heading Language

**Corporate headings (rewrite):**
- "Our Comprehensive Solution" → "What the platform actually does"
- "Key Features" → "The 6 things it handles for you"
- "Why Choose Us" → "How this compares to building it yourself"
- "Conclusion" → "The one thing to take away"

**Heading test:** Can a 15-year-old read this heading and know what the section is about? If no, rewrite.

### 6. AI Content Markers (Detect and Fix)

These phrases signal AI-generated content to Google raters and human readers:

| AI phrase | Human replacement |
|-----------|------------------|
| "It is important to note that" | Delete — just say the thing |
| "In conclusion" | Delete or "The short version:" |
| "Delve into" | "Look at" / "Explore" |
| "Utilize" | "Use" |
| "Facilitate" | "Help" / "Make easier" |
| "Optimize" (overused) | "Improve" / "Tune" / specific verb |
| "Landscape" (as noun for industry) | "Market" / "Space" / just the industry name |
| "Stakeholders" (without specifics) | Name the actual people |
| "Robust" | Describe the specific capability |
| "Seamless" | Describe what actually happens |
| "Leverage" (as verb) | "Use" / "Apply" |
| "State-of-the-art" | Delete — describe the actual feature |
| "Best-in-class" | Delete — use a real comparison |
| "Synergy" | Delete — describe the actual interaction |

### 7. Readability for AI Citation (GEO)

For AI systems (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) to cite a passage:
- The passage must be 134–167 words (optimal citation block size)
- It must open with a definition or direct answer
- It must contain at least one specific statistic or named source
- It must stand alone — readable without the surrounding context
- It must not start with "I" or the brand name

**Citation-ready block template:**
```
[Topic] is [one-sentence definition]. [Specific fact or statistic with source].
[1–2 sentences explaining the mechanism or why it works].
[Example with named specifics]. [One sentence on what to do next or what this means].
```

## Quick Wins

1. Replace all "In today's [adjective] landscape" openings
2. Swap every "leverage", "utilize", "facilitate" for a plain verb
3. Break any paragraph over 5 sentences into two
4. Add a specific number to every generic claim ("many" → actual count)
5. Move the subject to the front in passive sentences

## Medium Effort

1. Rewrite headings as questions or direct statements ("What X does" not "Our X")
2. Add one named example per major claim
3. Add Flesch-targeted sentence length editing pass
4. Create 1–2 citation-ready 134–167 word blocks per page

## High Impact

1. Add author byline with real name + credentials (not "Team")
2. Add first-person experience signals ("We ran this for 3 clients in 2024...")
3. Rewrite the opening 40 words to answer: what, who, outcome
4. Source every statistic with inline attribution

## Error Handling

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Content too short (< 300 words) | Flag as thin content. Humanization analysis limited. Recommend expanding first. |
| Content is entirely AI-generated boilerplate | Report count of AI marker phrases. Provide full rewrite of opening paragraph as example. |
| Technical/medical content | Adjust Flesch target to 40–55 for specialist audiences. Keep specificity rules. |
| Content behind authentication | Analyze only visible excerpt. Note limitation. Ask user to paste the full text. |
